TECHNOLOGY

Manual Technology

The Precision of Mechanical and Quartz Technology.

  • Battery-Powered Quartz Movements.
  • Self-Winding Movements (Automatic Watches).
  • Luxury Mechanical Movements.

Analog watches, rooted in traditional mechanical timepieces, utilize gears, springs, and escapements to measure time. The most fundamental element in analog watch technology is the escapement mechanism, which regulates the release of energy from the mainspring to move the watch hands. This mechanical precision is often regarded as the epitome of horological craftsmanship

However, modern analog watches predominantly feature quartz technology for higher accuracy and reliability. Introduced in the 1960s, quartz-powered watches replaced traditional mechanical components with a quartz crystal oscillator. When an electric current is applied to the quartz crystal, it vibrates at a precise frequency (32,768 times per second), and this vibration is used to generate accurate timekeeping. These watches are powered by a battery and typically require minimal maintenance, offering unparalleled precision with an average error rate of only a few seconds per month.

Digital Technology

The Evolution of Electronics and User Interface.

  • Smartwatches.
  • Solar-Powered Digital Watches.
  • High-Resolution Displays.

Digital watches, on the other hand, leverage the power of electronics and integrated circuits to provide a display of time in numerals. The core technology behind digital watches typically relies on LCD (Liquid Crystal Display) or LED (Light Emitting Diode) technology. In these watches, the time is generated by a quartz oscillator, similar to analog quartz watches, but the key distinction lies in the use of an electronic display to present the time rather than hands.

Digital watches often employ microprocessors and battery power, with modern variants incorporating additional features such as alarms, backlighting, GPS functionality, and fitness tracking. The introduction of the integrated circuit (IC) was a pivotal moment in digital watch development, allowing for the seamless integration of numerous features into compact, energy-efficient designs.
